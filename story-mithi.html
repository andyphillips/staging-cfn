Investing in people, restoring mangroves

MAY 04 2026 · 5 MIN READ

Coastal communities stand at the frontline of the climate crisis. Rising sea levels, extreme weather, and habitat loss threaten both livelihoods and biodiversity. But what if the solution isn't just about restoring ecosystems — it's about ensuring the people who depend on them have the tools, knowledge, and opportunities to lead the way?

That's the vision behind Project MITHI — Mangrove-based Initiatives for Thriving Habitats and Inclusive Communities.

Why mangroves? Why now?

Mangroves are one of nature's most powerful climate solutions. They absorb four times more carbon than tropical rainforests, shield coastlines from storm surges, and provide critical habitats for marine life. Yet they continue to disappear at alarming rates, often cleared for short-term gains like charcoal production.

As these Blue Carbon habitats are destroyed, centuries of stored carbon escape — exacerbating global warming and harming the most vulnerable people on the planet.

Three communities. One vision.

In its inaugural year, MITHI supports coastal communities in Aringay and Bauang (La Union) and Bais (Southern Negros) — alongside Oceanus Conservation, Security Bank and local stakeholders.

A community-led approach

The project was launched with conservation leaders including Nikki Lizares (VP and Sustainability Head of Security Bank), Rhoda Phillips (Chair and Founder of CfN), Camille Rivera (Director and Co-founder of Oceanus Conservation), local mangrove champion Delailah Lacadue and Engr. Rhea Tudado from the Aringay local government.

MITHI is more than just an environmental program. It's a collaborative effort to position communities as key conservation leaders, strengthening their role in habitat restoration, ecotourism, and sustainable livelihoods.

— Nikki Lizares, VP and Sustainability Head, Security Bank

As CfN Trustee Sef Carandang put it: "We need to invest in knowing the communities."

Security Bank Chief Finance Officer Eduardo Olbes added: "Our hope is to respond directly to the encouragement put forth by our partners. We hope to be tangibly participating and, from that, learning. It's only through us touching the ground and working side by side with individuals within the local community that our understanding will develop."
