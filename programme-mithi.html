Active Programme · Philippines

Project MITHI

Mangrove Restoration & Livelihoods — investing in people, restoring coastlines.

Mangroves absorb four times more carbon than tropical rainforests — and they're disappearing. MITHI restores them, with communities at the centre.

Why Mangroves

Nature's most powerful climate solution

Mangroves shield coastlines from storm surges, host critical marine life, and lock up centuries of carbon. When they're cleared, that carbon escapes — and the communities depending on them lose everything.

MITHI is restoring degraded coastal areas in Aringay, Bauang and Bais, with Oceanus Conservation, Security Bank and local stakeholders.

Community-Led Approach

Beyond restoration

Launched with conservation leaders including Nikki Lizares (Security Bank), Rhoda Phillips (CfN), Camille Rivera (Oceanus), local mangrove champion Delailah Lacadue and Engr. Rhea Tudado from Aringay's local government.

MITHI is more than just an environmental program. It's a collaborative effort to position communities as key conservation leaders, strengthening their role in habitat restoration, ecotourism, and sustainable livelihoods.

— Nikki Lizares, Security Bank

As CfN Trustee Sef Carandang put it: "We need to invest in knowing the communities."

Security Bank CFO Eduardo Olbes added: "It's only through us touching the ground and working side by side with individuals within the local community that our understanding will develop."
